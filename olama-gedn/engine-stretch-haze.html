How it works

Blur, drift, shimmer — in that order.

Three processes applied during the stretch phase, not after. The haze is intrinsic to the audio, not painted on top.

01

Spectral Blur Engine

Applies a controlled Gaussian blur across the spectral analysis frame before time-stretching synthesis. The blur depth controls how much frequency information "bleeds" into adjacent bins — simulating the diffusion of sound through space and material.

blur_mode: gaussian | depth: 0–100% | bands: 512
02

Micro-Pitch Drift

Each spectral band receives an independent slow-moving pitch modulation — driven by a low-frequency stochastic model. The drift is barely perceptible on any individual band, but collectively creates a rich, organic chorusing quality from within the stretched material.

drift_range: ±0.3st | rate: 0.05–0.8Hz | per_band: true
03

Harmonic Shimmer Layer

A gentle amplitude modulation applied to upper harmonic content only — creating a slow, breathing shimmer quality. Unlike shimmer reverb which adds pitch-shifted feedback, Haze's shimmer modulates harmonics that already exist in the source material.

shimmer_freq: 0.1–4Hz | harmonic_threshold: -18dBFS

Common questions

Can I use Temporal™ on a full mix bus?
You can, but it's most transformative on individual buses or stems — pads, vocals, atmospheric layers. On a full mix at high blur depth, it will obscure transient clarity. Use it on layers you want to push back in space, not the full mix.
Is there a CPU cost to the spectral blur?
Yes — Gaussian spectral blur is computationally heavier than standard time-stretching. At 512-band analysis, expect ~15–25% higher CPU usage than our other engines. The quality return is worth it. If CPU is a concern, freeze the track after processing.
What stretch ratios work best with Haze?
For subtle atmosphere: 1.05–1.3×. For strong atmospheric effect: 1.5–2.5×. For full transformation where the source is unrecognizable: 3–8×. At extreme ratios with high blur depth, Haze produces sounds closer to ambient synthesis than time-stretching.
