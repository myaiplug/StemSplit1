How it works

Three mechanics that make it feel analog.

Classic Engine isn't a filter on top of digital stretching. It models three distinct physical behaviors of tape-based time compression.

01

Harmonic Warmth Modeling

Adds even-order harmonic content that increases naturally as time ratio decreases — exactly what happens when tape runs slower than nominal speed. Result: automatic warmth injection proportional to your stretch amount.

saturation_curve: tape_2nd_order ↑ ratio: 0.72×
02

Transient Character Lock

Isolates transient energy before stretching and reinserts it post-processing. Snares stay punchy at 60% speed. Kicks retain attack. Hat rolls maintain groove. Your drum feel doesn't collapse when the tempo does.

transient_hold: enabled | attack_floor: 4ms
03

Low-End Density Fill

At stretch ratios below 0.85×, the algorithm automatically compensates for sub-frequency thinning — a known artifact in FFT-based stretching. Your bass stays full. Your kick has body. Nothing goes hollow.

sub_comp: adaptive | threshold: -18dBFS
